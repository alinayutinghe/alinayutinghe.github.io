PHONES, DRONES AND SATELLITES

Livestreaming—the real-time transmission of audiovisual content—facilitates the dissemination of information beyond geographical boundaries, fostering a sense of proximity and witnessing in security practices and global political communication. From terrestrial to extraterrestrial space, livestreaming has become a vital feature of mobile phones, drones, and satellites, introducing unprecedented levels of perceived authenticity, entanglement, and immediacy. My doctoral research investigates how livestreaming technologies reshape global security knowledge, practices and power, and the role of (non)-humans in the process of securitisation. This project builds on my previous works on ‘Battles on The ‘Fourth Front’: A Dromological Analysis of the First ‘TikTok W@r’, digital diplomacy practices on videoconferencing platforms and global digital politics.
